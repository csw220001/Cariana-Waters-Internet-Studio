Six Thinking Hats Discussion

Summary

Below is a summary of each hat's discussion:

White Hat

We have the information about what the website is and potential indie projects we could host on the website. We need to know how the information is going to be recorded and uploaded,
what defines an independent creator, what kind of media will be hosted here, and how it is monetized. Most of this we can figure out by decision, but when it comes to the definition of an
independent creator, that will be a combination of research and decision.

Red Hat

We are feeling hopeful and positive about the project. Our intuition says that it'll be a lot of reasearch to find projects to host on the site. As a whole, feelings range from some anxiety
towards scope to inspiration at the good it could do.

Black Hat

Potential problems include that the scope of this project may be too large for this class; the success of independent media is luck based, and so this website may not help so much; and
the scope of moderation of appropriateness of posted content may be too large.

Yellow Hat

This website has potential to be a great resource and is within the scope of possibility, and the masses are likely to appreciate and lift up a website like this. Even just sifting out
corporate media will give smaller creators a better chance. This project has value because it uplifts the voices of independent creators as well as connects different regions through the
sharing of art. Research will make this project work. Design and easy to use UI will be key in attracting users.

Green Hat

Instead of purely being a website where creators can post their work, maybe the admin team (me) can manually research and add pages for already-known indie projects. To solve the
black hat problems we need employees in order to run a website at the ideal scale.

Blue Hat

The subject of this discussion is my website idea. Our goal is to promote indie projects. Self check-in and reminders of the goal will keep us on track. The white hat cleared up questions,
the red hat was good morale as we looked at and listed the positives, the black hat felt necessary but upsetting to consider, the yellow hat felt as though it echoed red and white, and
the green hat was difficult but provided useful insights.

Changes

I plan for the website to be a mix of admin-created and creator-created pages showcasing works of indie media, with the added option for donations to keep the website (financially)
worth running. Additionally, if I continue to pursue this website following this class, I may look forward to gathering a team of volunteers to help me maintain and moderate it. Other than
these points, nothing much has changed with my project excepting the need for more research.
